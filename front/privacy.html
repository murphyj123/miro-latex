Privacy Policy

LaTeX for Miro — Last updated: 31 March 2026

Overview

LaTeX for Miro is a Miro app that lets you create beautifully rendered mathematical expressions directly on your Miro boards. We are committed to your privacy. This policy explains what data the app does and does not handle.

No Personal Data Collection

This app does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. There are no accounts, no analytics, and no telemetry.

No External Servers

All LaTeX rendering happens entirely client-side in your browser using MathJax, an open-source JavaScript library. Your LaTeX input is never sent to any external server.

Local Storage

The app uses your browser's localStorage solely to preserve editor state (such as your most recent LaTeX input) for convenience. This data:

  • Stays on your device
  • Is cleared at the start of each session
  • Is never transmitted anywhere

Miro SDK Permissions

The app uses the Miro SDK to interact with your board. It requests the following scopes:

  • boards:read — to read board context for positioning content
  • boards:write — to place rendered LaTeX images onto the board

These permissions are the minimum required for the app to function. The app does not access any other Miro data.

No Cookies or Tracking

This app does not use cookies, tracking pixels, fingerprinting, or any other tracking mechanisms.

Third-Party Libraries

The app uses MathJax for LaTeX rendering. MathJax is loaded and executed locally within your browser and does not send data externally. No other third-party services are used.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy is updated, the changes will be reflected on this page with an updated date.
